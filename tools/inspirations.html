Inspirations & Analogies

What it is

Deliberately importing solutions from elsewhere: how does nature solve filtration? How do restaurants handle peak-load queues? How did another industry crack an analogous problem? Analogical thinking is among the most reliable sources of genuinely novel concepts, because it breaks the frame the team has been staring at.

How to use it

  1. Abstract the problem to its functional core ('move many small things through a constrained channel').
  2. Hunt for domains that solve that abstract problem — biology, logistics, games, other cultures.
  3. Study how the analog works mechanistically, not just superficially.
  4. Translate the mechanism back into your context and sketch the result.

Watch out for

Surface-level analogies that borrow the image but not the mechanism. 'Like Uber, but for X' is a business-model analogy, not a design insight — make sure the underlying dynamics actually transfer.

Worksheet

Apply Inspirations & Analogies to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Ideate page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Ideate stage cards.
