Brainstorming

What it is

Semi-structured rapid idea generation in a group: a focused 'how might we' prompt, a fixed time box, judgment deferred, quantity encouraged, and building on others' ideas ('yes, and'). Done well it produces a wide option space fast; done badly it's a meeting where people defend their favorite idea.

How to use it

  1. Frame a specific 'How might we…' derived from the brief — narrow enough to aim at, broad enough to allow surprise.
  2. Individual silent generation first, then share and build.
  3. Go for volume; capture everything visibly; no criticism, no ownership.
  4. Cluster and label the output at the end — it feeds directly into concept selection in Make.

Watch out for

Stopping at the first satisfying idea. Push past the plateau — the second wave of ideas after the lull is often where novelty lives.

Worksheet

Apply Brainstorming to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Ideate page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Ideate stage cards.
