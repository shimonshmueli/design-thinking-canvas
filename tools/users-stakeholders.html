Users & Stakeholders

What it is

Mapping everyone the problem touches: primary users, secondary users, buyers, operators, regulators, and anyone else with a stake in the outcome. Users and stakeholders are not the same thing — the person who uses a hospital bed, the nurse who adjusts it, and the administrator who buys it have different needs that can conflict.

How to use it

  1. List every group that interacts with or is affected by the problem area.
  2. Distinguish users (who experience the problem) from stakeholders (who influence or are affected by the solution).
  3. Prioritize: whose needs will drive the design, and whose act as constraints?
  4. Plan research to reach the important groups directly, not through proxies.

Watch out for

Designing for the buyer while ignoring the daily user — or vice versa. Also beware 'user' groups defined by demographics rather than by their relationship to the problem.
