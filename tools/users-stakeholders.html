1.1 Users & Stakeholders用户与利益相关者

What it is

Mapping everyone the problem touches: primary users, secondary users, buyers, operators, regulators, and anyone else with a stake in the outcome. Users and stakeholders are not the same thing — the person who uses a hospital bed, the nurse who adjusts it, and the administrator who buys it have different needs that can conflict.梳理与该问题相关的所有人：主要用户、次要用户、购买者、操作者、监管者，以及任何在结果中有利益关系的人。用户和利益相关者并不是同一回事——使用病床的病人、调整病床的护士，以及采购病床的管理者，各自的需求可能互相冲突。

How to use it

  1. List every group that interacts with or is affected by the problem area.列出所有与该问题领域有互动或受其影响的群体。
  2. Distinguish users (who experience the problem) from stakeholders (who influence or are affected by the solution).区分用户（亲身经历该问题的人）与利益相关者（影响解决方案或受其影响的人）。
  3. Prioritize: whose needs will drive the design, and whose act as constraints?排定优先级：哪些人的需求将驱动设计，哪些人的需求只是约束条件？
  4. Plan research to reach the important groups directly, not through proxies.规划调研方式，直接接触重要群体，而不是通过代理人。

Watch out for

Designing for the buyer while ignoring the daily user — or vice versa. Also beware 'user' groups defined by demographics rather than by their relationship to the problem.只为购买者设计却忽视日常使用者——或者反过来。同时要警惕仅按人口统计特征而非「与问题的关系」来定义『用户』群体。
