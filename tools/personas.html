Define · tool / method

Personas

What it is

Composite, evidence-based characters that represent distinct user groups — each with goals, behaviors, frustrations, and context drawn from Discover research. Personas keep the team designing for specific people with specific needs instead of an elastic 'the user' who conveniently wants whatever the loudest person in the room wants.

How to use it

  1. Derive personas from behavioral patterns in your research, not demographics.
  2. Keep the set small — 3–5 — and mark one primary persona the design must serve.
  3. Give each one goals, pain points, context, and a telling quote from real data.
  4. Use them actively: 'would Maria understand this step?' should be a live question in every later stage.

Watch out for

Fictional personas invented without research are stereotypes with names. If you can't trace a persona attribute to evidence, cut it.

Worksheet

Apply Personas to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Define page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Define stage cards.
