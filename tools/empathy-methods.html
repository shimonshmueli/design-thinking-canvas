Empathy Methods & Tools

What it is

Techniques for understanding users' experiences from the inside rather than at a distance: empathy by simulation (recreating the user's conditions — e.g., wearing vision-impairment glasses), by immersion (living the experience yourself for a period), or by action (doing the user's tasks alongside them). Supporting tools include empathy maps, which organize what a user says, thinks, does, and feels.

How to use it

  1. Choose the cheapest method that gets you genuinely close to the experience.
  2. Record observations in the user's own words — resist paraphrasing into your assumptions.
  3. Capture emotional texture (frustration, pride, workarounds), not just tasks.
  4. Debrief immediately: what surprised you? Surprise is the signal of a broken assumption.

Watch out for

Simulation is an approximation, never the lived experience — treat it as a source of questions to verify with real users, not conclusions.
