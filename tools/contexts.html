Contexts

What it is

Studying where and when the problem occurs: the physical environment, the social setting, the workflow around it, the time pressures, the devices and tools present. Behavior that looks irrational in the abstract usually makes sense in context — people improvise around constraints researchers can't see from a conference room.

How to use it

  1. Go to where the activity actually happens; do not rely on recollection in an interview room.
  2. Document the environment: noise, interruptions, lighting, space, other people, competing demands.
  3. Note workarounds and improvised tools — they mark where existing solutions fail.
  4. Map the before and after: what triggers the activity, and what does the user do next?

Watch out for

Observing a sanitized or performed version of the context — people behave differently when watched. Spend enough time that normal behavior resumes.

Worksheet

Apply Contexts to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Discover page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Discover stage cards.
