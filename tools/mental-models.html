Mental Models

What it is

The internal explanations users carry about how something works — often incomplete or wrong relative to how the system actually works, but decisive for their behavior. A design that fights the user's mental model produces constant errors; one that matches it feels intuitive. Understanding the gap between the user's model and the system's model is a core Discover finding.

How to use it

  1. Ask users to explain or draw how they think the thing works.
  2. Watch for errors and hesitations — they mark where the model and reality diverge.
  3. Compare the user's model, the designer's model, and the system's actual behavior.
  4. Record the user's vocabulary; their words for things reveal their model's structure.

Watch out for

Correcting the user's model instead of learning from it. In Discover, a 'wrong' mental model is data, not a problem to fix.

Worksheet

Apply Mental Models to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Discover page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Discover stage cards.
