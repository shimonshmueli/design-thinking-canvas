Mockups

What it is

Static, visually faithful representations of the concept — screens, physical form studies, packaging — that show what the thing will look and feel like without full working behavior. Mockups converge the concept's form, attributes, and design language, and give stakeholders and test users something concrete to react to.

How to use it

  1. Move from low-fidelity wireframes/sketches to higher-fidelity mockups as decisions settle.
  2. Use realistic content — real labels, real data — because placeholder text hides real problems.
  3. Review against the brief's brand/design-language intent.
  4. Pair with prototypes: mockups answer 'is this right?', prototypes answer 'does this work?'

Watch out for

Polish too early. A pixel-perfect mockup of the wrong concept generates enthusiastic agreement to build the wrong thing.

Worksheet

Apply Mockups to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Make page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Make stage cards.
