UX & CX Evaluation

What it is

Assessing experience beyond task success: the user experience (UX) of interacting with the product — clarity, satisfaction, effort — and the customer experience (CX) across the entire journey around it: discovery, purchase, onboarding, support, renewal. A concept can pass task-level tests and still fail as an experience.

How to use it

  1. Instrument the full journey from the journey maps made in Discover — evaluate the same touchpoints.
  2. Combine behavioral measures (completion, time, errors) with attitudinal ones (satisfaction, perceived effort).
  3. Look for experience cliffs: single moments bad enough to undo everything else.
  4. Feed CX findings to the parts of the organization that own those touchpoints — many aren't 'the product'.

Watch out for

Averaging scores across the journey. A 4.5/5 average with one catastrophic touchpoint is a failing experience — users remember peaks and ends, not means.

Worksheet

Apply UX & CX Evaluation to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Evaluate page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Evaluate stage cards.
