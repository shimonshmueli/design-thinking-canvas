Competitive Analysis

What it is

A systematic look at how existing solutions — direct competitors, substitutes, and workarounds — address the problem space today. The goal in Discover is not to copy features but to learn what users already have, where current solutions fail them, and which needs remain unserved.

How to use it

  1. Inventory direct competitors, indirect substitutes, and the do-nothing / manual workaround option.
  2. Evaluate each against user needs, not feature checklists.
  3. Try competitor products yourself where possible; note where they frustrate you.
  4. Summarize the white space: needs no current solution serves well.

Watch out for

Feature-race thinking — cataloguing what competitors have and concluding you need it too. Gaps in the market matter more than parity.

Worksheet

Apply Competitive Analysis to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Discover page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Discover stage cards.
