Testing Methods & Criteria

What it is

The plan behind the testing: which methods (usability tests, A/B comparisons, field trials, technical benchmarks) answer which questions, and — critically — the pass/fail criteria defined before testing begins. Criteria come from the brief's success measures; defining them in advance is what keeps evaluation honest.

How to use it

  1. Return to the brief: what did we say success looks like? Turn each into a measurable test criterion.
  2. Match method to question: behavior → observation; preference → comparison; robustness → benchmarks.
  3. Decide sample, tasks, and thresholds before the first session.
  4. Report against the criteria, including the failures — especially the failures.

Watch out for

Moving the goalposts after results arrive. If the concept failed the pre-agreed criterion, the finding is 'it failed', not 'the criterion was wrong' — reframe consciously, not conveniently.

Worksheet

Apply Testing Methods & Criteria to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Evaluate page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Evaluate stage cards.
