2.8 Requirements Hierarchy需求层级

↓ Jump to workspace

What it is

Organizing what the solution must do into a structured tree — from high-level needs down through functional requirements to specific constraints — with priorities attached (e.g., must / should / could). The hierarchy connects every requirement to a user need above it, which prevents orphan requirements that exist because someone liked them.把解决方案必须做到的事项组织成一棵结构化的树——从高层次需求，向下延伸到功能需求，再到具体的限制条件——并附带优先级（例如：必须／应该／可以）。这一层级结构把每一项需求都与其上层的用户需求相连接，从而避免那些仅仅因为『有人喜欢』而存在的孤立需求。

How to use it

  1. Start from validated needs at the top; decompose into the functions that would satisfy them.从顶层已验证的需求出发；将其拆解为能够满足这些需求的功能。
  2. Attach measurable acceptance criteria at the leaves.在树的末端（叶节点）附上可衡量的验收标准。
  3. Prioritize explicitly (e.g., MoSCoW: must, should, could, won't).明确排定优先级（例如 MoSCoW 法：必须、应该、可以、不会）。
  4. Trace every requirement upward — if it doesn't serve a stated need, question it.把每一项需求向上追溯——如果它没有服务于某个已声明的需求，就要对它提出质疑。

Watch out for

Requirement inflation: everything becomes a 'must'. If more than ~a third of requirements are must-haves, the team hasn't converged.警惕『需求膨胀』：所有东西都被标记成『必须』。如果超过约三分之一的需求都是『必须项』，说明团队其实还没有真正收敛。
