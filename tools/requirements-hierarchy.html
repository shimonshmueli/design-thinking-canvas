Requirements Hierarchy

What it is

Organizing what the solution must do into a structured tree — from high-level needs down through functional requirements to specific constraints — with priorities attached (e.g., must / should / could). The hierarchy connects every requirement to a user need above it, which prevents orphan requirements that exist because someone liked them.

How to use it

  1. Start from validated needs at the top; decompose into the functions that would satisfy them.
  2. Attach measurable acceptance criteria at the leaves.
  3. Prioritize explicitly (e.g., MoSCoW: must, should, could, won't).
  4. Trace every requirement upward — if it doesn't serve a stated need, question it.

Watch out for

Requirement inflation: everything becomes a 'must'. If more than ~a third of requirements are must-haves, the team hasn't converged.

Worksheet

Apply Requirements Hierarchy to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Define page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Define stage cards.
