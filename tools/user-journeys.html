Customer / User Journeys

What it is

A visual map of the user's end-to-end experience across time: the stages they pass through, the actions they take, the touchpoints they hit, and how they feel at each step. Journey maps expose the pain points, gaps, and moments of delight that individual interviews mention only in fragments.

How to use it

  1. Define whose journey and what scope (one task? the whole relationship with a service?).
  2. Lay out phases left to right; under each, record actions, touchpoints, thoughts, and emotions.
  3. Ground every cell in research evidence, not team speculation.
  4. Mark the pain points and 'moments of truth' — these become candidate problem statements in Define.

Watch out for

Journey maps built from imagination in a workshop room are fiction with good graphic design. Evidence first.

Worksheet

Apply Customer / User Journeys to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Discover page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Discover stage cards.
