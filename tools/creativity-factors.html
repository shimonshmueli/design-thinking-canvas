Creativity Enablers, Inhibitors & Facilitators

What it is

The conditions under which groups actually produce novel ideas. Enablers include psychological safety, diverse perspectives, time to incubate, and clear constraints; inhibitors include premature evaluation, status hierarchies in the room, fear of looking foolish, and rewarding only 'realistic' suggestions. Designing the ideation session is itself a design task.

How to use it

  1. Set explicit rules that defer judgment — critique comes later, in Make.
  2. Mix disciplines and seniority levels; have people generate ideas silently before sharing (avoids anchoring).
  3. Use constraints deliberately: 'how would we solve this with no screen?' provokes more than a blank page.
  4. Watch the room: if only two people are talking, change the format, not the people.

Watch out for

The loudest-voice problem: unstructured group brainstorms reliably converge on the first confident speaker's idea. Structure protects divergence.
