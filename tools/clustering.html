Clustering & Affinity Mapping

What it is

Grouping raw observations into emergent themes. Individual data points (quotes, observations, pain points) are written on cards and sorted by affinity — what belongs together — until patterns appear that no single interview showed. It is the workhorse method for moving from data to insight.

How to use it

  1. Put each observation on its own card, phrased concretely.
  2. Sort bottom-up: group cards that feel related, then name each group after it forms — not before.
  3. Rearrange freely; early groupings are hypotheses.
  4. For each final cluster, write an insight statement: what does this pattern mean for the problem?

Watch out for

Naming categories first and sorting cards into them — that's confirmation, not discovery. Let the structure emerge from the data.

Worksheet

Apply Clustering & Affinity Mapping to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Define page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Define stage cards.
