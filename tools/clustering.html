2.1 Clustering & Affinity Mapping聚类与亲和图

↓ Jump to workspace

What it is

Grouping raw observations into emergent themes. Individual data points (quotes, observations, pain points) are written on cards and sorted by affinity — what belongs together — until patterns appear that no single interview showed. It is the workhorse method for moving from data to insight.将原始观察归纳为逐渐浮现的主题。把各个数据点（引述、观察、痛点）写在卡片上，按照『亲和性』——哪些内容属于一类——进行归类，直到出现单次访谈无法呈现的模式。这是从数据走向洞察的主力方法。

How to use it

  1. Put each observation on its own card, phrased concretely.把每条观察写在单独的卡片上，措辞要具体。
  2. Sort bottom-up: group cards that feel related, then name each group after it forms — not before.自下而上分类：先把感觉相关的卡片归为一组，等分组成形之后再命名——而不是先命名。
  3. Rearrange freely; early groupings are hypotheses.可以自由调整；早期的分组只是假设。
  4. For each final cluster, write an insight statement: what does this pattern mean for the problem?为每个最终的聚类写一条洞察陈述：这一模式对该问题意味着什么？

Watch out for

Naming categories first and sorting cards into them — that's confirmation, not discovery. Let the structure emerge from the data.先命名类别再往里塞卡片——这是在验证既有想法，不是在发现新知。要让结构从数据中自然浮现。
