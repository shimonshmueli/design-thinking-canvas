Back-casting

What it is

Ideating in reverse: define a desirable future state ('in five years, nobody misses a dose of medication'), then work backwards to identify what would have to exist — technologies, behaviors, services — to make that future real. Where forecasting extrapolates from today's constraints, back-casting frees the team from them.

How to use it

  1. Describe the target future concretely, from the user's point of view.
  2. Work backwards in steps: what must be true just before that future? And before that?
  3. Identify the earliest links in the chain — those are candidate solution directions available now.
  4. Compare against forward extrapolation to see which assumptions differ.

Watch out for

Utopian futures with no plausible chain back to the present. Every backward step should be an answerable 'what would it take?'
