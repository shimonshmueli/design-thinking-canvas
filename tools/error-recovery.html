Interactions & Error Recovery

What it is

Detailed evaluation of the interaction layer under imperfect conditions: what happens when the user makes a mistake, takes an unexpected path, or the system fails? Good designs prevent errors where possible, make them visible when they occur, and make recovery obvious and non-destructive. This is where robust concepts separate from fragile ones.

How to use it

  1. Enumerate likely error states: wrong input, interrupted flow, network/system failure, destructive actions.
  2. Walk each one: is the error prevented, clearly explained, and recoverable without data loss?
  3. Test with users who are rushed or distracted — error behavior under pressure is the real behavior.
  4. Verify undo paths and confirmation for destructive operations.

Watch out for

Only ever demoing the happy path. If the team has never watched the concept fail, Evaluate isn't finished.

Worksheet

Apply Interactions & Error Recovery to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Evaluate page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Evaluate stage cards.
