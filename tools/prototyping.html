Prototyping & Rapid Prototyping

What it is

Building preliminary embodiments of a concept to learn from — from paper sketches and cardboard mockups to 3D prints and clickable interface prototypes. The governing rule: build at the lowest fidelity that answers the current question. Fidelity should rise only as questions get finer.

How to use it

  1. Name the question first: what does this prototype need to teach us?
  2. Choose fidelity to fit the question — paper for flow, cardboard for ergonomics, code for performance.
  3. Build fast, keep it disposable, and put it in front of users early.
  4. Prototype in parallel: several rough alternatives teach more than one polished one.

Watch out for

Attachment. A prototype the team is proud of stops being an experiment and starts being defended. Keep them cheap enough to throw away.
