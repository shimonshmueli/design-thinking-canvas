Qualitative Research

What it is

Structured firsthand inquiry: contextual inquiry (interviewing users while they work, in their environment, letting their actions drive the questions) and ethnography (extended observation of people in their natural setting). Unlike surveys, qualitative methods surface the 'why' behind behavior and reveal needs users can't articulate directly.

How to use it

  1. Prepare open-ended questions but follow the user's lead — the plan is a starting point, not a script.
  2. Ask about specific recent events ('walk me through the last time…'), not generalities ('what do you usually…').
  3. Separate observation from interpretation in your notes.
  4. Continue until you reach saturation — new sessions stop producing new themes.

Watch out for

Leading questions and confirmation bias. If every finding confirms what the team already believed, the research design was probably flawed.

Worksheet

Apply Qualitative Research to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Discover page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Discover stage cards.
