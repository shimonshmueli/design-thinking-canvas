Internal Analysis (SWOT, Five Forces)

What it is

Framing the problem relative to your organization's position. SWOT maps internal strengths and weaknesses against external opportunities and threats; Porter's Five Forces assesses the competitive structure of the market (rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, supplier and buyer power). In Define, these tools check that the problem you're converging on is one your organization can credibly and viably pursue.

How to use it

  1. Run SWOT specifically about the candidate problem, not about the company in general.
  2. Use Five Forces to understand whether solving this problem creates a defensible position.
  3. Feed conclusions into the brief's constraints and point-of-view.
  4. Revisit the original challenge-selection criteria: does this still fit our values, objectives, role?

Watch out for

Generic SWOT lists ('strength: our people') that could apply to any company and constrain nothing. Every entry should change a decision.

Worksheet

Apply Internal Analysis (SWOT, Five Forces) to your project and capture what you find. Entries are saved in your browser, appear under "From tool worksheets" on the Define page, feed the AI context there, and can be sent up as Define stage cards.
