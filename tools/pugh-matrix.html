Pugh Matrix (Concept Selection)

What it is

A structured concept-selection method from engineering design (Stuart Pugh). Candidate concepts are compared criterion-by-criterion against a datum (reference) concept, scored better (+), same (0), or worse (−) on each criterion drawn from the brief's requirements. The point is less the arithmetic than the disciplined, criteria-driven argument it forces.

How to use it

  1. Choose criteria from the brief: needs, constraints, success measures.
  2. Pick a datum — often the strongest existing solution.
  3. Score every concept +/0/− against the datum on each criterion; discuss disagreements, don't average them away.
  4. Look for hybrids: a concept weak overall may carry one brilliant element another concept can absorb.
  5. Iterate with the best concept as the new datum.

Watch out for

Score-worship. The matrix is a conversation structure; if a result contradicts the team's informed judgment, interrogate the criteria and weights, not just the winner.
