Stage 4 · Convergent · Solution space

Make

Realization and representation — converge on concepts and make them tangible.

Make is the convergent half of the solution-space diamond: the wide option set from Ideate is filtered, combined, and developed into a small number of concepts made real enough to test. 'Real enough' is the key phrase — the output is not a finished product but a Solution Concept (SC): a tangible representation (prototype, mockup, MVP) plus a description of its structure, functions, interactions, and form.

Selection and realization interleave here: rough filters (like a Pugh matrix) narrow the field, prototyping makes survivors concrete, and what you learn building often loops back — solution refining — into another pass of ideation or selection.

Comes in

The candidate solution directions from Ideate.

Goes out

One or more Solution Concepts (SC): structure & morphology · functions, features, behaviors · user interactions · form, attributes, brand/design language — embodied in testable prototypes.

Solution Concept — the exit artifact

  • Structure, morphology
  • Functions, features, behaviors
  • User interactions
  • Form, attributes, brand/design language

Tools & methods for this stage

Pugh Matrix (Concept Selection)

A structured concept-selection method from engineering design (Stuart Pugh).

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

The smallest version of a solution that lets you test its core value hypothesis with real users at real stakes.

Prototyping & Rapid Prototyping

Building preliminary embodiments of a concept to learn from — from paper sketches and cardboard mockups to 3D prints and clickable interface prototypes.

Mockups

Static, visually faithful representations of the concept — screens, physical form studies, packaging — that show what the thing will look and feel like without full working behavior.

Synthesis (Concept Integration)

The convergent act of combining the surviving elements — selected ideas, prototype learnings, mockup decisions — into one coherent Solution Concept: an explicit description of the concept's structure and morphology, its functions and behaviors, its user interactions, and its form.
