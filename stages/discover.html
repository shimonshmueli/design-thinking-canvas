Stage 1 · Divergent · Problem space

Discover

Research & empathy — explore the problem widely before deciding what it is.

Discover is the first, divergent half of the problem-space diamond. You enter it with a Challenge — a broad territory the team has chosen to work in — and your job is to resist solving anything yet. Instead, you go wide: talk to users and stakeholders, observe real behavior in real contexts, question assumptions, and collect raw material about what is actually going on.

The thinking modes here are exploratory: collecting, imagining, expanding scope, splitting problems apart. Both secondary research (what's already known — literature, market data, prior studies) and primary research (what you learn firsthand — interviews, observation, immersion) belong here. The quality of everything downstream depends on the honesty and breadth of this stage.

Comes in

A selected Challenge (C) — screened against your values, objectives, role, and business fit (e.g., via SWOT).

Goes out

A rich, unfiltered pool of observations, quotes, artifacts, and insights — the raw material Define will make sense of.

Tools & methods for this stage

Users & Stakeholders

Mapping everyone the problem touches: primary users, secondary users, buyers, operators, regulators, and anyone else with a stake in the outcome.

Empathy Methods & Tools

Techniques for understanding users' experiences from the inside rather than at a distance: empathy by simulation (recreating the user's conditions — e.g., wearing vision-impairment glasses), by immersion (living the experience yourself for a period), or by action (doing the user's tasks alongside them).

Contexts

Studying where and when the problem occurs: the physical environment, the social setting, the workflow around it, the time pressures, the devices and tools present.

Qualitative Research

Structured firsthand inquiry: contextual inquiry (interviewing users while they work, in their environment, letting their actions drive the questions) and ethnography (extended observation of people in their natural setting).

Customer / User Journeys

A visual map of the user's end-to-end experience across time: the stages they pass through, the actions they take, the touchpoints they hit, and how they feel at each step.

Mental Models

The internal explanations users carry about how something works — often incomplete or wrong relative to how the system actually works, but decisive for their behavior.

Competitive Analysis

A systematic look at how existing solutions — direct competitors, substitutes, and workarounds — address the problem space today.
