Stage 5 · Testing

Evaluate

Test the solution concept with users, customers, and experts.

Evaluate is where the Solution Concept meets reality before serious money does. The concept is tested from multiple angles — with the users it's meant to serve, with domain experts, and against established standards and heuristics — using criteria defined back in the brief. The stage's honest outputs are evidence and decisions: what works, what fails, what to refine, and occasionally what to reframe entirely.

Findings loop backwards by design: small failures drive solution refining (back into Make/Ideate), and fundamental failures drive problem reframing (back into Define). Passing Evaluate feeds the Develop gate, where viability, feasibility, and desirability are weighed before committing to build.

Comes in

The Solution Concept (SC) and the brief's success criteria.

Goes out

Evidence-backed verdicts against the success criteria; a refined concept ready for the viability/feasibility/desirability check in Develop.

Tools & methods for this stage

Testing Methods & Criteria

The plan behind the testing: which methods (usability tests, A/B comparisons, field trials, technical benchmarks) answer which questions, and — critically — the pass/fail criteria defined before testing begins.

Customer / User Testing

Putting the prototype in front of real users and watching them attempt real tasks with it.

Expert Panels & Heuristic Evaluation

Structured review by people with deep relevant expertise: domain specialists, and usability experts walking the interface against established heuristics (e.g., Nielsen's ten), guidelines, and standards (accessibility, safety, industry regulations).

UX & CX Evaluation

Assessing experience beyond task success: the user experience (UX) of interacting with the product — clarity, satisfaction, effort — and the customer experience (CX) across the entire journey around it: discovery, purchase, onboarding, support, renewal.

Interactions & Error Recovery

Detailed evaluation of the interaction layer under imperfect conditions: what happens when the user makes a mistake, takes an unexpected path, or the system fails? Good designs prevent errors where possible, make them visible when they occur, and make recovery obvious and non-destructive.

Stress & Quality Tests

Pushing the concept to its limits: peak load, extreme environments, edge-case data, sustained use, misuse.
