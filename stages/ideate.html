Stage 3 · Divergent · Solution space

Ideate

Creating potential solutions — go wide again, now inside the frame of the brief.

Ideate opens the second diamond. The Problem Definition is fixed; the solutions are not. The team diverges again — generating many candidate directions, deferring judgment, combining and stretching ideas — before Make converges on the ones worth building. Quantity genuinely matters here: the first ideas are usually the obvious ones, and the interesting territory starts after they're exhausted.

The brief acts as the guardrail: wild ideas are welcome, but they must aim at the stated problem, users, and success criteria. Ideas that reveal a flaw in the brief itself are valuable too — that's the problem reframing loop back to Define.

Comes in

The Problem Definition (PD) — problem statement, needs, point-of-view, constraints, success criteria.

Goes out

A broad, documented set of candidate solution directions, ready for filtering and realization in Make.

Tools & methods for this stage

Creativity Enablers, Inhibitors & Facilitators

The conditions under which groups actually produce novel ideas.

Brainstorming

Semi-structured rapid idea generation in a group: a focused 'how might we' prompt, a fixed time box, judgment deferred, quantity encouraged, and building on others' ideas ('yes, and').

Back-casting

Ideating in reverse: define a desirable future state ('in five years, nobody misses a dose of medication'), then work backwards to identify what would have to exist — technologies, behaviors, services — to make that future real.

Technology Inventory

A systematic scan of technologies — mature, emerging, and adjacent-industry — that could serve the problem.

Design Heuristics

Reusable idea-generation moves distilled from studying how experienced designers create concepts — transformations like scale it up/down, merge two functions, invert the interaction, make it modular, change what powers it, let the user reconfigure it.

Inspirations & Analogies

Deliberately importing solutions from elsewhere: how does nature solve filtration? How do restaurants handle peak-load queues? How did another industry crack an analogous problem? Analogical thinking is among the most reliable sources of genuinely novel concepts, because it breaks the frame the team has been staring at.
