Stage 6 · Gate check

Develop

Weigh viability, feasibility, and desirability — decide whether and what to build.

Develop is the gate between a validated concept and a committed development effort. The classic innovation test applies: a concept worth building sits at the intersection of desirability (people genuinely want it — evidenced by Evaluate), feasibility (it can actually be built with available technology, skills, and quality — evidenced by prototyping and stress tests), and viability (it sustains itself as a business — pricing, cost, channel, strategic fit).

The honest outcomes here are go, no-go, or go-back: fund development, kill the concept, or loop back into the process with what was learned. Weak concepts that pass this gate on enthusiasm cost far more to kill later.

Comes in

The evaluated Solution Concept, with evidence against the brief's success criteria.

Goes out

A build/no-build decision — and, on go, a development plan that carries the concept's intent into engineering, production, and launch.

Focus areas

  • Desirability — do target users demonstrably want this? What does the Evaluate evidence actually show?
  • Feasibility — can we build it, at the required quality, with the technology and capabilities we have or can get?
  • Viability — does the business model close? Cost, price, channel, and fit with strategy and brand.
